Dance

My youth was filled with dance classes, recitals and competitions. It was my first love and passion and I sought to try every dance genre and style from ballet, breakdancing, tap, jazz to disco. After attending full time dance school, I focused my efforts on the contemporary style and was able to perform as a professional in numerous shows and at events. I also consistently taught and mentored both children and adults with a variety of schools and clubs. Although my career came to end, my joy of dance continues.

Dance Fitness

Easy-to-follow choreography that keeps participants moving in an effort to raise their heart rates. It is a fun way to burn calories, explore your creative side, tone muscles and clear your mind.

Contemporary

Contemporary is an artistic and expressive combination of dance techniques, usually including floor work and showcases agility and free ranging movement.

Jazz

Jazz is a combination of dance styles that comes from the musical theatre arts. Jazz takes techniques from Classical Ballet and modern dance and uses isolation and strength to emphasize the movement. Jazz has its own movement vocabulary and wims to use the entire body alongside the accents of musical rhythms.

Broadway

Broadway dance can be defined as the incorporation of ballet, jazz and modern dance styles with theater and singing. The use of theater props such as canes, top hats and gloves is also very popular in Broadway productions, allowing once again a variety of movements previously unexplored in theater or dance productions.

Funk

Funk dancing is seen as more modern, however is the perfect fusion of styles, incorporating jazz roots into a melting pot of hip hop, break, popping and locking. As you would expect, it is danced with funk music and eases into a groove.
